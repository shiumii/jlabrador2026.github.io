Kenshi Yonezu

(米津 玄師 Yonezu Kenshi, born March 10, 1991), also known by his producer name Hachi (ハチ), is a Tokushima-born artist, musician, singer-songwriter, and VOCALOID song producer and illustrator. He started producing VOCALOID original songs around May 2009. Before this, he had already been producing music. The songs which he composes have a unique point of view, which is common in his songs. Similar to his musical works, his illustration style is also very characteristic, using a scribbled style, often combining abstract, surrealist, and urban elements.

Cien Rojas, nee Cien Miller

Also known as Crusher, Cien M, Omega Destroyer Dictator Crusher, and Bunny Lord, and formerly known as Crusher-P, debuted in August 2010. She is an American songwriter, music producer, singer and illustrator. In the VOCALOID community, she was well-known for her fantasy-themed songs with ethereal, eerie, and intricate sound works.

DECO*27

(デコ・ニーナ Deko Niina, born December 16, 1986) is a Fukuoka-born VOCALOID song producer and utaite. His first work got over 100,000 view counts quite suddenly, and his current popularity is stable. His style has been described as a fusion of rock and pop. He had a sub-name called DEKOUS*UK when cooperating with kous and sasakure.UK in ef's album (恋色クローバーピース). DECO*27 also stretches his limits outside of VOCALOID, having composed songs for various anime and video games. In 2020, he is in charge of the interactive music project MILGRAM.

Eve

Eve (いぶ ibu), also known as Keitora (けいとら) and Kurowa (クロわ), is mostly known as an utaite on the platforms Niconico and YouTube. However, around the mid-2010s, he had also starter composed original VOCALOID works. Although he has little experience as a VOCALOID producer, he has gained huge success, as all of his VOCALOID works have entered the Hall of Fame and all except sister have entered the Hall of Legend. His most notable songs include "Nonsense Bungaku" and "Dramaturgy", both of which have exceed 3,000,000 views on Niconico. He was born on May 23rd.

His YouTube channel is linked in the image.

Jin

Jin (じん), also known as Shizen no Teki-P (自然の敵P), born October 20, 1990, is a producer whose works are mainly rock. He mainly uses IA and Miku. He is best known for the Kagerou Project series, which got several novel and manga adaptations, as well as an anime adaption named Mekakucity Actors. Jin also composed the opening and ending theme of the above mentioned anime. His notable songs include "Kagerou Daze", "Children Record", "Lost Time Memory", "Night Tales Deceive", and "Outer Science".

His YouTube channel is linked in the image.

Pinocchio-P

(ピノキオピー) is a VOCALOID producer known mainly for the fast paced and unique lyrics in his songs, including several unique characters in his PVs such as Aimaina and Doushite-Chan. He mainly uses Miku, though he also uses Rin, GUMI, Yukkuri, and MAYU. His most notable songs are "Kamippoi na", with over 40,000,000 YouTube views, and "Slow Motion". His producer name comes from his very first song "Hanauta" which means "humming" (and is written with the kanji meaning "nose" and "song").

wowaka

(ヲワカ), also known as Genjitsutouhi-P (現実逃避P), stemming from his video descriptions that "sometimes it's good to escape from reality.", debuted in May 2009. He was one of the founders of the Balloom record label, along with other Vocalo-P, Hoehoe-P, Toku, OSTER project, scop, Hachi, Agoaniki-P, and Fullkawa Honpo. In 2011 he formed the band hitorie and remained active there. On August 22, 2017, he returned to VOCALOID with "Unknown Mother-Goose". On April 5, 2019, he died of heart failure at the age of 31.

HarryP

(はりーP), also known as Tsubasa Harihara (針原翼) or simply Harry (はりー), is a VOCALOID producer from Kyoto, Japan who primarily uses Hatsune Miku. His most notable work to feature a VOCALOID is Bokura no Let-it-Be. In parallel with his activities in the VOCALOID field, HarryP is also the vocalist and guitarist of a band named ROZEO EMBLEM. (often indicated as "Harry's band"). HarryP also composes music for various singers and bands, most notably Wolpis Carter.
